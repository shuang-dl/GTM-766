8. Analysis & findings

Executive summary

Over March 1 – June 19, 2026, the 21‑member Customer Support team scored 96.1% CSAT (2,612 of 2,717 ratings positive), about 7 points above Fin AI at 89.1% (3,018 of 3,387). When every human teammate across all teams is included, human CSAT is 91.8%. The clearest opportunity is not satisfaction quality but survey response volume: surveys are sent on most conversations (up to ~75%), yet only about 10% of conversations return a rating. Fin's satisfaction improved notably in June (to ~92.5%), narrowing the gap with humans.

Verified facts (read directly from Intercom reports)

  • CONFIRMED Customer Support team CSAT (21 members): 96.1% (2,612/2,717). Sub‑groups: CS primary 96.9% (1,999/2,063), Inbound Calls Team 93.5% (575/615).
  • CONFIRMED Fin AI CSAT: 89.1% (3,018/3,387). All‑teammate CSAT (all teams): 91.8% (3,019/3,287). Overall (humans + Fin): 88.0% (6,121/6,954).
  • CONFIRMED Monthly request rate (vs. all conversations): 53.9% → 62.4% → 75.7% → 73.2% (Mar→Jun). Monthly response rate: 10.5% → 11.3% → 11.6% → 9.2%.
  • CONFIRMED Channel CSAT (overall): Chat 90.6%, Email 88.6%, Phone 90.9%. Teammate‑only: Chat 92.4%, Email 88.3%, Phone 91.9%.
  • CONFIRMED Conversation volume (type‑is‑User): 59,683 total; ~79% involved Fin; 56% Fin‑resolved; 34% closed by a teammate.
  • CONFIRMED Negative ratings (Terrible + Bad): 304 total — 151 on human agents, 147 on Fin (~6 chatbot). Team SLA miss rate 73.8% (11,057/14,985); team median first response 2m.
  • CONFIRMED Weekly CSAT is volatile; Fin's strongest week was the week of Jun 1 (~93.4%), right after ~85.7% the prior week — the monthly "June jump" is one spike, not a steady climb.
  • LIKELY Response rate by channel (read from chart): Chat ~11%, Phone ~9%, Email ~4%. By segment: all ~10–12%. CSAT by topic: 86–94% across all topics.

Analysis

Humans outperform Fin on satisfaction, but the gap is closing. The support team's 96.1% sits well above Fin's 89.1%. However, Fin's monthly CSAT rose from ~88.8% (March) to ~92.5% (June) — its strongest month — while the support team held steady in the high‑90s. LIKELY If Fin's June trend holds, the human‑vs‑Fin satisfaction gap will keep narrowing.

The real lever is response rate, not score. Request rate climbed all period (more conversations are being surveyed), but only ~1 in 10 conversations returns a rating. That means every reported CSAT score — for humans and Fin alike — rests on a relatively small, self‑selected slice of customers. Raising response rate would make all of these numbers more reliable and would surface more of the dissatisfied customers who currently stay silent.

Channel differences are small. Chat (the primary channel, including in‑app) and Phone both land around 91–92% for humans; Email is a few points lower at 88.3%. LIKELY Email trails slightly because it handles more complex billing/cancellation issues (visible in the email topic mix) and lacks the real‑time back‑and‑forth of chat and phone.

Per‑teammate spread is healthy, and the phone team runs a touch lower. Across the 21 Customer Support team members, almost everyone sits between 94% and 100%; the lowest score on meaningful volume is 88.5% (Jonny Diaz, 78 ratings). Broken out by group: the 11 CS‑primary agents average 96.9%, while the 6 Inbound Calls (phone) Team members average 93.5% — a ~3.4‑point gap. LIKELY The phone team scores lower because spoken calls tend to handle harder, real‑time issues, which lines up with the channel view in Section 4. The 4 secondary‑only members (97.4%) have too few ratings to read into.

Speed and CSAT are loosely linked, not lockstep. The slowest first‑responders include Jonny Diaz (15m 15s) and Diana (14m 48s); Jonny Diaz also has the lowest CSAT on real volume (88.5%), which fits, but Diana still scores 94.2% — so slow replies don't automatically sink satisfaction. Fast responders like Paolo G (1m 14s) and Oliver Z (31s) post top‑tier CSAT. NOTE The team SLA miss rate is high at 73.8%, but it doesn't track cleanly with CSAT (e.g., Frank misses 79% of SLAs yet scores 97.3%), suggesting the SLA targets themselves may be mis‑set rather than satisfaction being SLA‑driven — worth a separate look.

Email is a near‑silent channel. Only ~4% of email conversations get rated, versus ~11% on chat and ~9% on phone. LIKELY That means email's 88.3% CSAT rests on a much thinner base than chat's — and that raising email survey response is the cheapest way to learn more about the lowest‑scoring channel. Response rate is essentially flat across customer segments (~10–12%), so who responds is driven by channel, not company size.

No single topic is dragging CSAT down. Across Intercom's AI topics, CSAT sits in a tight 86–94% band. The lowest is the broad "Custom / Status / Tasks" catch‑all (86%) — likely a grab‑bag of harder, less‑templated requests rather than one fixable issue. LIKELY The clearer pattern lives in the negative comments. The 304 negatives split almost evenly — 151 on human agents, 147 on Fin — but for different reasons: human‑agent complaints are about issues not getting resolved ("Problem still isn't fixed," "my question was not addressed"), while Fin complaints are about wanting a person ("I need a live agent please"). So the two fixes are different: better resolution/closure for human conversations, and a smoother path to a human when Fin is stuck.

Read CSAT weekly, not just monthly. The weekly view shows both lines bounce around in the mid‑80s to low‑90s. Fin's encouraging "June jump" in the monthly chart is really a single strong week (week of Jun 1, ~93.4%) immediately after a dip to ~85.7% — so it's too early to call it a durable improvement. LIKELY Watching the weekly line through July will show whether Fin holds above ~90% or reverts to the ~88% it has averaged.

  • Monthly request/response‑rate values and the monthly human‑vs‑Fin line were read from chart tooltips/labels; aggregate figures and the per‑teammate table were read from numeric report tables (higher precision).

  • Email and Phone Fin CSAT (100% and 66.7%) rest on a handful of ratings and are not statistically meaningful. Slack and SMS had no CSAT ratings at all.
  • "June" is a partial month (through the 19th), so June volumes are lower than full months by construction.
  • Response rates by channel and segment were read from each filtered monthly response‑rate line (no single aggregate number is exposed), so they are approximate to within ~1–2 points.
  • CSAT‑by‑topic uses Intercom's AI conversation topics. Topic names are auto‑generated (some are internal‑sounding) and a conversation can match multiple topics, so topic "volume" overlaps and should be read as relative size, not a clean count.
  • The negative‑rating count (304) is for Terrible + Bad with type‑is‑User and excludes the small "Phone switch" channel; most negative ratings carry no written comment, so the themes come from the minority that do. The human (151) / Fin (147) / chatbot (~6) split uses Intercom's "Agent rated type."
  • Weekly CSAT values were read from the Fin Surveyed CSAT "by week" chart (approximate, ±1–2 pts), and the first plotted week (Mar 23) is partial. The conclusion — Fin's June lift is one spike week — holds regardless of small reading errors.
  • First‑response‑time and SLA‑miss figures are scoped to the same Customer Support + Inbound Calls team filter as the CSAT, so they line up with the per‑teammate CSAT in the table.

Recommended next steps

  • Lift CSAT response rate. At ~10%, response volume is the weakest link. Test survey timing, wording, and placement (e.g., in‑app prompts) to get more customers to rate.
  • Watch the Fin trend monthly. Fin's June jump to ~92.5% is worth confirming next month to see whether the gap with humans keeps closing.
  • Look at Email specifically. It is the lowest‑scoring channel; reviewing low‑rated email conversations may reveal a fixable pattern (response time, tone, or topic complexity).
  • Support the Inbound Calls (phone) team. At 93.5% they trail CS‑primary agents (96.9%) by ~3 points. Reviewing low‑rated calls — or comparing them to chat handling — may surface coaching or process opportunities specific to phone.
  • Smooth the bot‑to‑human handoff. The clearest reason behind negative ratings is customers wanting a human ("I need a live agent please"). Making it faster/clearer to reach a person when Fin can't resolve should lift the worst ratings — and ties directly to Fin's 22% escalation rate.
  • Review the SLA targets. A 73.8% miss rate that doesn't correlate with CSAT suggests the SLA thresholds may be set too tight to be meaningful. Worth confirming the targets reflect real expectations before treating misses as a problem.
  • Treat low‑volume teammate scores with care. Don't act on a teammate's CSAT or response‑time until they have a reasonable number of ratings (the rows tagged "low n", e.g. Jodi Medina, Torez, Matt J).
