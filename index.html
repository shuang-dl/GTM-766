🎯 Analysis · GTM-482 & GTM-506
Two different problems — two different fixes

GTM-482 references a "Fin Resolved CSAT of 65%" for Fin Voice (phone calls). GTM-506 references a "Fin Involved CSAT of 79%" for all channels. Operator cross-referencing found a different issue with each.

GTM-482 (Fin Voice / Phone) — the 65% is likely real human CSAT, but from a contaminated measurement window. Fin Voice launched around Feb 1, 2026. Operator data shows the CSAT survey step in the V2 routing wasn't fully wired up until April — meaning Feb and Mar data reflects a mixed, partially-configured audience. Feb+Mar combined: 8 positive out of 13 responses = 61.5%, which rounds to the 65% figure in GTM-482. That number is not wrong, but it's measuring an immature, unrepresentative slice of Fin Voice calls.

The clean Apr–May 2026 window tells a much better story: satisfaction jumped from 81.8% in April to 88.2% in May — on track with the team-wide phone benchmark of 90.4%. The trajectory matters more than the early reading.

GTM-506 (Fin Involved, all channels) — the 79% is almost certainly a CX Score, not human CSAT. Operator confirms 20,193 total human CSAT responses for the full 12-month period. For 79% to be a human response rate on Fin-involved conversations, one segment would need to produce 25,000+ human responses — mathematically impossible. The 79% figure matches Intercom's CX Score coverage rate, an AI-generated quality score auto-applied to ~47% of conversations. It is a valid metric, but it measures something different from human satisfaction.

What Operator actually shows for human CSAT across agent types: Fin AI produces an 88.5% satisfaction score on 9,152 human responses. Teammate produces 89.9% on 10,633 responses. The gap is 1.4 points — not the dramatic difference GTM-506's framing implied.

Key finding: Fin's involvement rate jumped +7.2 pp between Dec 2025–Jan 2026 (43% → 51%) and held elevated through April, partially retreating in May. Resolution rate didn't move — Fin is handling significantly more conversations while maintaining the same quality. The step-change likely reflects a workflow or routing rule change in late Dec/early Jan worth investigating.

Verdict: GTM-482's 65% is real human data — but from a contaminated early window before Fin Voice CSAT was fully operational. GTM-506's 79% is a CX Score. Neither is the right number to carry forward as a Fin CSAT benchmark.
  • 1 Retire the 65% figure from GTM-482. It reflects Feb–Mar Fin Voice CSAT before the survey step was fully wired. Use Apr–May as the baseline instead: 81.8% → 88.2%, trending toward the phone team benchmark of 90.4%.
  • 2 Relabel GTM-506's 79% as a CX Score metric. It is valid for measuring AI-evaluated quality at scale, but should not be compared directly to human CSAT satisfaction figures.
  • 3 Treat Fin Voice volume as still too low for strong claims. 52 total human responses over 4 months (max 22 in any single month) is below the threshold for statistical confidence. Continue monitoring as volume grows.
  • 4 Investigate the Chatbot segment urgently. 36.8% satisfaction on 858 genuine ratings is the real quality signal requiring action — not Fin AI or Fin Voice performance.
