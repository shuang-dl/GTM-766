Methodology note: V2 uses a different denominator than V1 Intercom does not expose a standalone "surveys sent" count in the CSAT rating system. In this report, response rate = CSAT responses ÷ conversations where the customer sent at least one message (100,064 total). This denominator is larger than "surveys sent," producing a lower percentage than V1's figures — but it is the most accurate available measure. Surveys-sent data is not available for this period.
📉
Response rate is not computable per agent type — Cut 3 shows CSAT scores only Intercom does not break down the "conversations where customer sent a message" denominator by rated-actor type (Fin AI vs. Teammate vs. Chatbot). Cut 3 therefore omits response rate and shows CSAT responses and satisfaction scores only. This is a system limitation, not a data gap in the analysis.

🎯 Analysis · GTM-766
Two critical signals — a declining trend and a quality crisis

The 3-month window (Mar 1 – Jun 18, 2026) produces two findings that exceed the GTM-766 acceptance criteria: a sharp decline in Fin AI involvement rate over the period, and a Chatbot CSAT score so low it constitutes the #1 unresolved quality problem in the workspace. Both are addressed here alongside the GTM-482 and GTM-506 representativeness verdict.

GTM-482 (Fin Voice / Phone) — the 65% was not representative. Current phone CSAT is 89.9%. GTM-482 cited a "Fin Resolved CSAT of 65%" sourced from Feb–Mar 2026 before the Fin Voice CSAT survey step was fully operational. V2 data confirms the correction: phone channel CSAT for Mar 1 – Jun 18 is 89.9% on 486 genuine responses — on par with the team-wide phone benchmark. The early figure should not be carried forward as a baseline.

GTM-506 (Fin Involved, all channels) — the 79% was a CX Score, not human CSAT. Current Fin AI CSAT is 89.1%. V1 identified that the 79% figure matched Intercom's AI-generated CX Score, not human satisfaction ratings. V2 confirms: Fin AI human CSAT is 89.1% on 3,370 responses — robust enough for a strong benchmark claim. The gap between Fin AI (89.1%) and human teammate (91.9%) is just 2.8 points, consistent across both the 3-month and 12-month windows.

Cross-reference resolved — this is a mix-shift artifact, not a Fin performance regression. The Operator denominator includes ALL conversation types (Chat, Phone, Email, chatbot-only, internal). As non-Chat volume grew from ~48% to ~58% of total conversations over the period, the Operator's overall involvement rate declined mathematically — even though Fin's actual Chat involvement never changed. The "Fin AI Agent (New)" custom report (Chat + USER only — Fin's primary domain) confirms involvement was 95.2% overall and stable every month: Mar 95.6%, Apr 95.1%, May 95.3%, Jun 94.8%. Absolute Fin-involved Chat conversations declined (14,160 → 7,307) because total Chat volume declined — not because Fin was deployed less within Chat. Resolution rate on Chat is also stable at 71.8% (vs. 69.5% all-channel from Operator). CSAT confirmed across both sources at 89.1–89.2%.

Anomaly 2 — Chatbot CSAT: Critically Low at 30.3%

Chatbot (sole) handled 31,145 conversations over 3 months — the highest single-category volume in the period — and produced a 30.3% CSAT score on 268 genuine human ratings. This is not a small-sample finding. At 268 ratings, the signal is robust. Chatbot volume exceeds teammate-only volume (12,624) by more than 2.5×, making this the highest-leverage quality improvement opportunity in the workspace. V1 found the same issue at 36.8% over 12 months — the problem has not improved.

Verdict: GTM-482's 65% and GTM-506's 79% were both unrepresentative. V2 provides the corrected benchmarks.
  • 1 Retire GTM-482's 65% Fin Voice baseline. Current phone CSAT is 89.9% on 486 responses. The early figure reflected an immature, partially-configured measurement window and is no longer relevant.
  • 2 Relabel GTM-506's 79% as a CX Score (AI-evaluated), not human CSAT. The real Fin AI human CSAT benchmark is 89.1% on 3,370 responses — use this going forward.
  • 3 Monitor the Chat-USER volume trend — confirmed not a Fin performance issue. Cross-referencing the Operator against the "Fin AI Agent (New)" custom report confirms that Fin's Chat involvement is stable at 95.2%. The Operator's -11.7 pp decline is a denominator effect: non-Chat volume grew as a share of total conversations. Track whether Chat-USER volume as a share of total continues to decrease, and assess whether that channel mix shift is intentional or a drift.
  • 4 Urgently audit Chatbot workflows. 30.3% CSAT on 31,145 conversations is the #1 quality problem — not Fin AI. This is a persistent issue (V1 also flagged it at 36.8% over 12 months) with no evidence of improvement.
